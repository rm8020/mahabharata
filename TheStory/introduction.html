My Method

I am evolving my method but as of now, I am just using a couple of sources which itself are huge. I am also using google to search certain terms, location and people whom I am not familiar with to gain more understanding. Mahabharta is divided into the 18 Parvas and I will also use the same divisions for the sections but I may take more liberty with the chapters.

Though I have a general understanding of the story I have never read the complete text before so to write my own adaptation my options are basically

a) read all the sources and then start writing this text leading to-

  • a better,
  • consistent and
  • more comprehensive text
  • with less number of interactions

b) read a section and write about that. This option means

  • my text may not be consistent,
  • I may know accurately know what I am writing as it's just the first reading of my source text,
  • I will need a lot of iterations and corrections
  • but this option means I can start immediately and this means I may not lose interest.

I have chosen Option B so that I can start immediately. The option also has a better chance of me to continue to have an interest in the project (I lose interest fast and I have chosen a two-year project!)

For me, the intention is to improve my own understanding of the world by the way of Mahabharata. Mahabharata is the ultimate and the largest EPIC and by no means, I can have a first draft ready in less than 18 months. I am targeting 2 years to finish the first draft.
